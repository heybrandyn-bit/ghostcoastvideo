PROJECT BRIEF: Ghost Coast Video

Brand Identity: Ghost Coast
Design Standard: E-180 High-Grade VHS Aesthetic
Core Objective: To turn long-form archived data into high-velocity "Information Packets" (Video + Web) using a unified code-driven factory.
1. Visual Identity System (The E-180 Design DNA)

The aesthetic is based on professional analog master tapes from the early 90s. It balances high-impact commercial energy with technical, industrial precision.
A. The Master Palette
Element	Hex Code	Brand Role
VHS Yellow	#FFC800	Highlights, Scarcity Bars, Accent Text
VHS Orange	#FF6D00	Secondary Progress Stripes, Warning Labels
VHS Red	#E61D5F	Primary Headlines, "REC" Indicators
VHS Purple	#5E2D5E	Technical Badges, Footers, "Pro" Accents
Cream White	#F3F3E9	"Label" Backgrounds, Reading Sections
Pro Black	#1A1A1A	Main Background, Frame Texture
B. Typography Hierarchy

    The Command (Sans): Inter (Weight 900). All-caps, tight tracking (-0.05em). Used for aggressive, high-conversion headlines.

    The Heritage (Serif): Playfair Display. Italicized. Used for "Simply," "The," or sub-headlines to add a "boutique" or prestigious feel.

    The Technical (Mono): JetBrains Mono or VCR OSD Mono. Used for labels, timestamps, and "Tape Spine" data points.
